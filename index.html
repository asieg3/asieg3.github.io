01 About

A planner's mindset, an analyst's toolkit.

My path into financial planning started with anxiety. Heading into college, money stressed me out and I didn't really understand it — so I went down a rabbit hole. The more I learned about a few core financial principles, the more that anxiety turned into confidence. And confidence turned into a genuine passion for helping other people feel exactly that.

That passion became a finance degree, then a career — building multi-year forecasting models in Excel and OneStream at Johnson Controls, and now running inventory and reporting analytics at Advocate Aurora, where my work helped drive $3.6M in revenue growth.

But numbers only matter if people can use them. Four years of high-volume, client-facing roles taught me to translate complex financial information into clear, trustworthy guidance — the heart of good planning.

So I started building the tools you'll see below — to manage my own money, prepare for the CFP®, and show how I think: model it, make it beautiful, make it usable. The goal behind all of it is simple — help people build a stronger financial future.

05 Insights

Writing is the job.

Good planning is mostly good communication — making the complex feel simple and the scary feel manageable. Here's how I think about money, in my own words.

Essay
June 2026 · 2 min read

The principle that turned my financial anxiety into confidence

When I started college, money scared me. Not because I didn't have enough of it — because I didn't understand it. Every decision felt like it might be the wrong one, so I avoided thinking about it at all. That's the worst thing you can do, and it's exactly what most people do.

What changed everything wasn't a hot stock tip or a budgeting app. It was one boring idea: focus only on what you can control. You can't control the market, interest rates, or what happened last year. But you can control your savings rate, your expenses, and how early you start. Those three levers do almost all the work.

Once I stopped trying to predict the future and started controlling my inputs, the anxiety turned into a plan — and the plan turned into confidence.

That reframe is the whole reason I want to do this for a living. Most people aren't looking for someone to beat the market. They're looking for someone to take the fear out of the room and replace it with a clear next step. I've felt that shift myself, and I've built the tools — a budget system, a net-worth tracker, a live dashboard — that made it real for me.

Helping someone go from "I don't want to look at it" to "I know exactly where I stand" is the most valuable thing I think I can do. That's the job.

— Adam Siegel
